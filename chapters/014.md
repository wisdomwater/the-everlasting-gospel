## 14 - The Honest Mirror

> *True humility begins when we stop pretending our need for mercy is smaller than anyone else’s.*

When we look closely at the world—and even more honestly at ourselves—we begin to see how deep disorder can run. Words wound easily. Anger flares. Fear drives us to harm, exclude, and curse even our own lives. This is not said to condemn, but to awaken tenderness. Without mercy, the sight would be unbearable.

There is something quietly healing in admitting our shared brokenness. When we stop ranking ourselves above others—whether humans or unseen beings—we soften. We recognize that what opposes Love is not “out there” alone, but entangled within the human heart. This recognition does not crush us; it clears the ground for humility.

What resists God most deeply is not ignorance, but the will that insists on standing alone. The impulse to be self-sufficient, self-justifying, untouched by dependence—this is the fracture Love works to heal. And it is precisely here that mercy meets us first, not because we deserve preference, but because compassion always moves toward need.

When we finally see ourselves truthfully, we also begin to see the immensity of the remedy. Grace is not a small medicine for small wounds. It is vast because the illness is deep. And this same grace does not stop with us—it reaches as far as the disorder reaches, refusing to abandon any place where healing is still possible.

Sit gently with this question:

> *What shifts in you when you allow humility to replace comparison?*
